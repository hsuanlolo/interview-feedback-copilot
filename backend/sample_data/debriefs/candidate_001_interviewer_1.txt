Overall this was a strong technical screen. Jordan clearly has a solid grounding in statistics and I would recommend advancing to the full loop.

On the A/B testing design question, Jordan immediately asked about the traffic split and the minimum detectable effect before designing anything. That is exactly the right instinct. They correctly computed the required sample size (walked through the power calculation step by step) and flagged that the test needed to run for at least two weeks to capture weekly seasonality. I was impressed that they brought up seasonality without prompting — most candidates do not think about that.

They explained why: VIP users are not representative, so including them would bias the estimate upward. The explanation was clear and correct.

Communication was good. Jordan structured responses well — started with the core point, then elaborated. Occasionally went a bit deep on math before checking whether I was following, but adjusted when I signaled. I wouldn't call this a concern, just something to watch.
